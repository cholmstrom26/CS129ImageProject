We investigate the problem of estimating the time of day from a single outdoor webcam image. Using an expanded SkyFinder dataset of 49,280 images across 53 static cameras, we develop and evaluate a progression of models (brightness-based regression baselines, polynomial and histogram feature models, and deep convolutional neural networks based on ResNet-18 transfer learning with circular output encoding). The dataset was expanded from an initial 8-camera subset used in our milestone to address significant overfitting. The larger, more diverse 53-camera dataset substantially improved generalization. Models are evaluated via 5-fold group cross-validation on dev cameras, with final scores reported on 8 completely held-out test cameras. Our best CNN model achieves a test circular MAE of 4.251 hours after outlier-based training data cleanup, outperforming all baselines, although still struggling with overfitting to general day and night.
